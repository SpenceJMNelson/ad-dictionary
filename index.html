About the Project
✕

Finding Education
& Elightenment in Play

Arbor is a music/educational app that is meant to instruct people on the many species of trees living in North America, as well as provide a framework for beginning to understand deeper time. More broadly, the project explores the relationship between play and leaning, and asks the question whether a game can provide a catalyst for the development of a more complex understanding of the world around us.

The idea for this project came out an interest in the species of trees encountered in urban environments, originating from all over the world. Native species like the oaks and Maples, and Sycamore, and introduced species like Goldenrain, Crepe Myrtle, and Tree of Paradise mark our cities. Additionally, it seems that (as many of the ways humans impact the planet can’t be felt from one year to the next) in order for people to feel compelled to change their behavior, they first need to perceive longer spans of time. instilling a sense of longer spans of time. By viewing the growth of trees highly accelerated, one can begin to imagine the trees in the real world as following the same motions over a much longer timescale.

For this dictionary, the terms have been organized according to a taxonomy that tries to get at the different levels of interaction possible with the product itself. "Reverie" refers to the surface-level playful interaction with the product as a game only. The creation of a musical arrangement. "Fact" Refers to the second level onteraction, where the user begins to differentiate between the species of trees, learning information about them as they play. Finally "Insight" refers to the deeper understanding a user will hopefully have when encountering these same tree species in the real world: a more visceral sense that they are watching not something static, but something in motion, in the process of changing and becoming.
